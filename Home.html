Zardon Vision

Our Story

One afternoon at the library, we noticed a young boy struggling to read a novel. After approaching and speaking to him, we learned that he was visually impaired and had forgotten his glasses at home. After hearing about this, we were determined to use our programming skills to create an innovative app to help him and others like him. We developed Zardon Vision to utilize the mobile device’s camera to scan the surroundings to assist the visually impaired. We designed the app to have a clean and user-friendly interface, with large high-contrast buttons and clear instructions. Our aim was to create an app that would not only help the young boy we met at the library, but also others facing similar challenges.

Object Recognition

By utilizing the device's camera, the app can scan the surroundings and identify people and objects. We used a custom GPT-2 and PyTorch model. An image can get uploaded to our Flask server where it is identified and then it sends the information back to the app which reads out what the image was out loud. This feature would allow the user to understand his/her surroundings and navigate their environment with ease.

Text Recognition

Zardon can also take a photo of text to help a visually impaired person understand what it says. Using the Apple AI vision api, the app can automatically identify the borders of where the text starts and ends. The OCR software uses pattern-matching algorithms to compare text images, character by character, to its internal database. If the system matches the text word by word, it is called optical word recognition. The app uses advanced optical character recognition (OCR) technology to scan and interpret text. With this technology, the app can capture text and convert it into audio, providing an immediate and accessible way for individuals to comprehend written communication such as books, documents, and signs.

Depth Perception

Disparity is the key concept behind perceiving depth. When observing an object, your eyes capture slightly different views, known as disparity. The brain utilizes this disparity to determine the object's distance. The iPhone Front camera employs a similar approach using two cameras and analyzing "feature points." Through a triangulation algorithm, the iPhone can compute the 3D position of each feature point, enabling it to determine the depth of objects within the scene. This technique proves valuable for various applications like augmented reality and portrait mode photography as it allows the iPhone to gauge the distance to different objects in the scene. This technology can allow visually impaired people to more easily navigate their surroundings without exterior aid.

Facial Recognition

Zardon Vision can also classify humans. By using a custom trained Yolov2 + Pytorch model we can somewhat accurately classify different people. Some difficulties we faced were overfitting(which occurs when a statistical model fits exactly against its training data.), inaccurate, no catch case(has to be either one or the other)

Siri GPT

We used IOS shortcut platform to program an easy way for visually impaired people to get access to information quickly. Our app provides a link to the shortcut where users can set it up in less than 10 seconds. A user can then activate Siri and say “Hey Siri, Mini Helper”. You can then speak a request to a custom trained model based off of GPT-3 and Siri will then speak out the information that the model gave verbally.

Help Center

This page can help any visually impaired person receive help when they require it. They have the option of receiving help from a call center for general needs or specialized medical help from a hospital. We’ve also integrated a contacts page which allows any visually impaired person to promptly contact another person.

Technologies

We used a combination of Swiftui and UIkit for the frontend. We used Python, Flask, and PyTorch for the image recognition, For the text recognition, we used an Apple API called VisionKit and AVFoundation. We utilized html and css for the website. For the facial recognition, we used YoloV2, Create Ml, and pytorch.

Struggles

For this project, we used mostly Swiftui. This is a newer framework that had a lot of issues. Especially with the camera so we had to use uikit for that stuff. This was also the first time the majority of our group had ever used Swift/App development. Additionally, the design process ended up being rather difficult. We had to consider the demographic that the app was designed for (visually impaired) and make sure that it could be easily used by them. We went through many different designs for our app and website till we came up with something that would work.

Impact

This app provides solution that enhances accessibility and has the power to revolutionize the lives of the visually impaired. Zardon Vision offers a new level of convenience by allowing the user to conveniently access written information. Moreover, the app has the potential to save lives. Object recognition and navigation assistance provide users with increased safety. Furthermore, this app serves as a precedent for future innovation by showcasing how this technology is capable of helping visually impaired people.
